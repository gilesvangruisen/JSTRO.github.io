About

Welcome to my Capstone portfolio for the UM Minor in Writing! My name is Joe Ostrow, and I'm a senior concentrating in Biopsychology, Cognition & Neuroscience (BCN) with minors in both Writing and Spanish.

The centerpiece of this portfolio is my Capstone Project, which is an exploration of musical creation both from a personal perspective and from the perspective of professional musicians. It is hosted on Medium.com and can be found below.

Also showcased here are several other pieces I have written ranging in subject from psychology to anthropology, and in genre from academic review to personal narrative. While they each stand alone as complete pieces, in the context of this portfolio my hope is that they can reinforce an idea that my project introduces: the importance of learning through reference, reusing and recycling content, and the sharing of ideas.

Capstone Project

It’s been said before but it’s worth repeating: there are no completely new ideas, just new implementations of those ideas. In the world of art especially, creation never occurs in a vacuum; rather it thrives on inspiration, borrowing, and stealing. It is the chaotic product of a community who are in constant referential experimentation.

In this project I focus on the realm of music, and how and why this process of creation comes to be. Much of my information comes directly from the mouths of musicians, because after all this process is fundamentally an individual one. Nevertheless, I argue that woven across different genres and motivations is a universal thread to which all humans can connect. This connection and the way it manifests is what I hoped to explore with this project. Click here to view it.

Other Writing

My Evolution as a Writer

An analysis of personal adaptation through my writing. Specifically, I focus on the continuing process on achieving credibility with my work, what I believe this means, how I hope to accomplish it, and why I think it is important.

"On The Run" Review

My review of Alice Goffman's recent urban ethnography, "On The Run," written after six years of field work, which documents police surveillance and brutality in a primarily black, working class neighborhood in Philadelphia.

Turnbull Paper Critical Review

My critical review of Colin Turnbull's 1961 documentation of the unique perceptual abilities of the Mbuti pygmies of the then-Belgian Congo.
